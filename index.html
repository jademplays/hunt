The Four Dimensions

Each axis is scored independently from 1–10. A game doesn't need 10s across the board, but imbalances matter.

H Handling

Mobility Under Resistance

How motion, combat, and interaction feel when the game pushes back. Speed helps, but this is control under constraint.

  • Movement is gameplay
  • Precision and recovery are learned
  • Mastery improves flow, not just output

U Unlocks

Capability Expansion

Whether progression changes what you can do, not just how hard numbers hit. Unlocks must open space, not just fill bars.

  • Unlocks add verbs
  • Build identity emerges
  • Progression creates new problems

N Novelty

Epistemic Openness

How long the game stays unknown. Novelty is not content volume. It's unexhausted possibility.

  • Systems resist total understanding
  • Meta evolves through play
  • Players argue about "the right way"

T Tell

Visibility of Mastery

Whether skill leaves visible, legible traces. This is not competitiveness. This is recognizability.

  • Mastery announces itself
  • Style emerges
  • Spectating reveals competence

Design Philosophy

HUNT doesn't ask if a game is "good." It asks if a game gives you something to hunt.

Agency > Content

The ability to act meaningfully matters more than the volume of things to do.

Learning > Consumption

Discovery through play is more satisfying than checking off content lists.

Mastery Changes Perception

Skill should transform how you see the game, not just your damage numbers.

Systems Resist Comprehension

The best games continue teaching without tutorials, revealing depth over time.

Warning Patterns

Imbalances that lead to burnout, boredom, or isolation

High H + Low U

→ Feels good, burns out

High U + Low H

→ Progression treadmill

High N + Low T

→ Interesting but isolating

High T + Low N

→ Solved meta, stagnation

Sustainable games cluster at 7+ in three axes, with Handling never below 6.
